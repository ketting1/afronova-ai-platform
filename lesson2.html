✍️ Lesson 2: Prompt Engineering for Beginners

How to talk to AI and get what you want

🤔 What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt Engineering is the art of asking AI the right questions to get the best answers.

Think of it like giving instructions to a very smart assistant. The better your instructions, the better the results!

💡 Simple definition: Prompt Engineering is learning how to talk to AI so it understands exactly what you want.

🎯 Why Does It Matter?

  • Get better answers: A good prompt gives you a good response.
  • Save time: No more re-writing the same question over and over.
  • Unlock AI's full potential: Most people use AI at 10% – good prompts unlock 90%.
  • It's a superpower: Prompt Engineers are in high demand right now.

📝 The 3 Parts of a Good Prompt

1. 🎯 Role / Persona

Tell the AI who it should be.
Example: "Act as a professional chef..."

2. 📋 Task / Goal

Tell the AI exactly what you want it to do.
Example: "...write a 5-step recipe for a chocolate cake..."

3. 📐 Format / Constraints

Tell the AI how you want the answer.
Example: "...in bullet points, using simple language."

🧠 Full example: "Act as a professional chef. Write a 5-step recipe for a chocolate cake in bullet points using simple language."

💬 Prompt Examples

❌ Bad Prompt (Vague)

"Write about AI."

Problem: Too vague – the AI doesn't know what you want.

✅ Good Prompt (Specific)

"Act as a technology teacher. Write a 3-paragraph introduction to Artificial Intelligence for high school students. Use simple language and include real-world examples."

Why it works: Role, task, audience, format, and examples are all clear.

🔄 Prompt Chaining (Advanced)

"First, explain what AI is. Then, give 3 examples of AI in daily life. Finally, list 2 risks of AI."

Why it works: Break big tasks into smaller steps.

💡 Tips for Better Prompts

  • Be specific: "Write a poem" is vague. "Write a 4-line poem about friendship" is better.
  • Give examples: "Write in this style: [example]"
  • Tell AI what NOT to do: "Don't use jargon. Don't repeat yourself."
  • Iterate: If the answer isn't perfect, refine your prompt and try again.
  • Ask for different formats: "Give me the answer as a table / checklist / dialogue."

🔬 Challenge: Your First Prompt

Try this right now:

Prompt: "Act as a coding teacher. Write a simple HTML page that shows a 'Hello World' message. Include a button that says 'Click Me' that shows an alert."

What to do: Copy this prompt, paste it into an AI (like ChatGPT), and see what it gives you. Then try to improve the prompt!

🚀 Next level: Add more constraints: "...use a dark theme with purple accents and make the button round."
